Tests whether two categorical variables are associated. A p below alpha means they are related; Cram\'{e}r's V gives the strength of association (0--1); each cell's standardized residual (r) beyond about $\pm$1.96 flags a category combination that significantly drives the association. Valid only when expected counts are mostly $\ge$ 5 (the app flags this and suggests Fisher's exact) and each case appears once (raw counts, not percentages). Your significance threshold ($\alpha$) is 0.05.

A chi-square test of independence gave $\chi$\textsuperscript{2}(2, N=40)=4.90, p = .086, V=.35 [.00, 1.00].
