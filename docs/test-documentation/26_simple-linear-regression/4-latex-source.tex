R\textsuperscript{2} is the share of outcome variance explained. The predictor's B is the slope (change in outcome per unit), with p testing whether it differs from zero and the CI showing precision. $\beta$ is the standardized slope (change in outcome in SDs per 1 SD change in the predictor), useful for comparing effect sizes on a common scale. Your significance threshold ($\alpha$) is 0.05.

A simple linear regression gave B=0.64, t(38)=8.58, p $<$ .001, R\textsuperscript{2}=.66.
