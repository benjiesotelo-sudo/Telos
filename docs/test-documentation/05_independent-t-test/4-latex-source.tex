\includegraphics{figures/01_independent-t-test/figure_boxplot.png}

Asks whether two groups' averages differ by more than chance. Look at p: below alpha (e.g. 0.05) means significant. The mean difference and 95\% CI show the size and precision of the gap; Cohen's d gives effect size (\textasciitilde{}0.2 small, 0.5 medium, 0.8 large). A large p means no significant difference was detected --- not that the group means are equal.

An independent-samples t-test compared control (M=70.3, SD=3.1) and treatment (M=82.3, SD=3.8), t(9.68)=$-$5.98, p $<$ .001, d=$-$3.45 [$-$5.32, $-$1.53].
